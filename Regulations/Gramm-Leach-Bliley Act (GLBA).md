# GLBA (Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act) – High-Level Overview

---

## What is GLBA?

- **Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act (GLBA)**, also known as the Financial Services Modernization Act of 1999
- U.S. federal law requiring financial institutions to protect the **nonpublic personal information (NPI)** of individuals

---

## Who Does GLBA Apply To?

- **Applies to:** Individual consumers seeking financial products/services for personal, family, or household use
- **Does NOT apply to:** Organizations or individuals seeking products/services for business, commercial, or agricultural purposes

---

## What Does GLBA Protect?

- **Nonpublic Personal Information (NPI):**
  - Personally identifiable, nonpublic financial info collected by a financial institution
  - **Does NOT include:** Publicly available information

### Examples of NPI:
- Name, address, date of birth
- Social Security Number, driver’s license number
- Account numbers, credit card numbers
- Account balances, payment history
- Income history, credit rating
- Tax return information
- Info provided on loan applications
- Info obtained from consumer reports
- Info resulting from transactions (e.g., assigned account numbers)
- Lists/descriptions/groupings derived from NPI (e.g., list of loan holders)

---

## Why is GLBA Compliance Important?

- **Penalties:**
  - Organizations: Up to $100,000 per violation
  - Officers/Directors: Up to $10,000 per violation and 5 years in prison
- **Reputational risk:** Loss of public trust

---

## Key GLBA Rules

### 1. Safeguards Rule
- Requires information security program to prevent unauthorized access to NPI

### 2. Financial Privacy Rule
- Regulates collection/disclosure of NPI
- Requires privacy notice to consumers

### 3. Pretexting Rule
- Prohibits obtaining/disclosing NPI under false pretenses or deception

---

## Your Responsibilities

- Follow all policies and procedures to protect the privacy and security of NPI
- Help ensure compliance with GLBA
